Q1 REVIEW

What worked

  • Account growth: 81% increase in paying accounts. People are buying the product
  • Onboarding: New flow lifted campaign creation rates from 31.8% to 48.8%
  • CRM cross-sell: +21% uplift in new CRM Enterprise, 5x higher upgrade rate, churn drops from 8.7% to 3.1%
  • Workflows: Automation triggers opened up new use cases beyond one-off campaigns
The CRM cross-sell data is strong evidence that campaigns is a retention tool for the broader business, not just a standalone product.

Q1 REVIEW

What didn't

  • ARR miss: Target was $1.5M, tracking to ~$1.24M
  • Activation gap: 51% of paying accounts have never sent a campaign
  • Slowing velocity: New paying accounts dropped from 213 (Jan) to 91 (Mar partial)
  • March downgrades: 47 downgrades vs 22 in prior months. Cause unknown
The activation gap is the thread running through most of these problems. If people aren't sending campaigns, they're more likely to churn or downgrade.

THE BIG PROBLEM

51%
of paying accounts have never sent a campaign

New onboarding improved creation rates (31.8% → 48.8%) but the create-to-send gap is still wide. Draft-only rate sits around 79%. Half our revenue base is at risk.

This is the single biggest problem we need to solve. It drives churn, undermines retention metrics, and means half the accounts paying us aren't getting value. Q2 has a dedicated Adoption pillar to address this.

COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

The market is moving fast

  • ActiveCampaign launched "Active Intelligence": AI agents for all customers, custom instructions, MCP server connecting to Claude/ChatGPT/Slack
  • Mailchimp doubled down on ecommerce with predictive analytics and a ChatGPT integration
  • HubSpot shipped a Canva integration and ChatGPT CRM connector

AI is moving from "feature" to "operating model". Feature expectations (A/B testing, lead scoring, advanced automation) are becoming table stakes.

ActiveCampaign is the most relevant competitive move. They're positioning as "first to launch AI that acts, not just answers", which directly challenges our AI-first narrative. We need to move on our agentic vision.

OUR FORMAT

Good Strategy, Bad Strategy

Good strategy is not a list of goals. It's a kernel with three parts:

1. Context

An honest diagnosis of the challenge we face

2. Policy

A clear approach that channels effort and guides decisions

3. Action

Coherent actions that are coordinated and focused

Richard Rumelt, Good Strategy Bad Strategy (2011)

This is our strategy format. Context first (what's actually happening), then a guiding policy (how we'll approach it), then coherent actions (what we're actually doing). The next slides follow this structure.

CONTEXT

The challenge

We are growing accounts but failing to activate them, whilst the competitive landscape accelerates around us.

  • 51% of paying accounts have never sent a campaign
  • AI is shifting from assistive to agentic across the market
  • The Suite direction has pivoted to a leaner model
  • The team is growing but still constrained (14 people)
This is the Rumelt diagnosis. Four factors define our situation. The activation gap is number one. The competitive shift to agentic AI means we can't wait. The Suite pivot simplifies our architecture work. And we need to be ruthless about what we take on given team size.

POLICY

Two additions to our playbook

Agentic > Assistive

Moving from Sidekick (AI helps you) to agents (AI does things for you). Starting with Campaign Research and Landing Page agents

Foundations alongside expansion

Building new channels in parallel with strengthening the base. When they compete for resources, foundations take priority

Agentic AI is how we respond to ActiveCampaign's move and stay ahead. Foundations alongside expansion means we won't sacrifice stability for features, but we'll move fast on both when we can.

FOCUS

What we're not doing

  • Full CRM Suite add-on model (decided, out of scope)
  • Building for B2C or ecommerce (that's Mailchimp and Klaviyo's territory)
  • Competing on API breadth

At risk / v0 only: Performance marketing and ads. Given constraints, we'll aim for something very lean

Being clear about what we're not doing is just as important as what we are. The ads decision is still open. If we do it, it'll be a very minimal version.
